About Children's Tribe

A diverse, educational community rooted in the Montessori philosophy.

We foster curiosity, creativity, and critical thinking in every student — on a 13-acre working farm in Godfrey, Illinois.

Our Mission

Educating the whole child.

Our mission is to provide a complete Montessori program that considers the emotional, intellectual, physical, and social development of the total child — addressing the uniqueness of each in a nurturing, secure, and academically excellent environment.

We believe every child deserves a place where they are deeply seen, meaningfully challenged, and invited to contribute as a full member of a caring community.

Our Approach

Freedom within limits. Follow the child.

The Children's Tribe program is based on children's developmental needs and creates freedom within limits through a carefully prepared environment.

01

Prepared Environments

Beautiful, purposeful classrooms organized at child-scale, with materials that invite focused, independent work.

02

Mixed-Age Cycles

Three-year classrooms where younger children learn from older peers, and older children teach what they know.

03

Long Work Cycles

Uninterrupted three-hour blocks so children can enter deep concentration — the birthplace of real learning.

04

Guides, Not Teachers

Trained adults who observe each student and offer the right lesson, at the right moment, to move them forward.

05

Grace & Courtesy

Daily practice of kindness, contribution, and care — the foundation of a thriving community.

06

The Land as Teacher

Gardens, greenhouse, farm animals, and a student-run Farmers Market woven throughout the curriculum.

Accreditation & Affiliations

A serious, accredited Montessori program.

  • Registered with the Illinois State Board of Education
  • Member of the American Montessori Society (AMS)
  • AMS- and AMI-aligned classroom practices
  • Multi-age Montessori cycles from Primary through High School

Children's Tribe serves learners from Pre-K through 12th grade — a rare continuity that lets families stay rooted in one community from the earliest years through graduation.
