Programs

A Montessori education that grows with your child.

From Primary through High School — plus a farm curriculum, before/after care, and an eight-week Summer Program.

Primary Lower Elementary Upper Elementary Middle School High School Farm Curriculum Before & After Care

Ages 3 – 6 · Half or full day

Primary (Children's House)

Our Primary program is half or full day, with 3-, 4-, 5-, and 6-year-olds interacting in the same carefully prepared environment. Children move through the classic Montessori curriculum areas — practical life, sensorial, language, mathematics, and cultural studies — at their own pace, guided by trained Montessori educators.

  • Half-day AM: 8:00 – 11:30
  • Half-day PM: 12:00 – 3:00
  • Full-day option available
  • Gentle kindergarten year as a capstone

Grades 1 – 3

Lower Elementary

First, second, and third graders learn together in the same environment. The curriculum is organized around the Great Lessons of Montessori cosmic education, sparking wonder and tying every subject together into one story of the universe, life, and human work.

  • Integrated study across math, language, geometry, history, biology, and geography
  • Small-group collaborative projects and research
  • "Going out" work connecting the classroom to the community
  • Weekly farm work and stewardship responsibilities

Grades 4 – 5

Upper Elementary

Fourth and fifth graders share a mixed-age classroom, deepening their research, writing, and mathematical fluency while taking on greater responsibility for the community. Students plan trips, lead initiatives, and begin to see their work as meaningful contribution.

Grades 6 – 8

Middle School

A mixed-age classroom for 6th, 7th, and 8th graders — Montessori's "third plane" learners. Adolescents engage with rigorous academic work, entrepreneurship on the farm, and meaningful service, preparing them for high school with confidence and self-knowledge.

Grades 9 – 12

High School

Our high school runs as a mixed-age community of 9th, 10th, 11th, and 12th graders — a true erdkinder model where students take on substantial work on the land and in the world, while preparing thoroughly for college, careers, and citizenship.

All grades

Farm Curriculum

Our 13-acre campus is a working farm. Students get hands-on horticulture in the on-site greenhouse and garden areas, and care for a variety of farm animals as part of the curriculum.

Students work together to run the school's Farmers Market, and many create their own small business to take to market — learning economics, biology, and stewardship through real responsibility.

  • Greenhouse and garden horticulture
  • Animal care and farm chores
  • Student-run Farmers Market
  • Student-founded micro-businesses
  • Seasonal, place-based curriculum

Wraparound care

Before & After Care

We offer before, after, and summer care options to support working families. Children enjoy quiet work, outdoor play, farm time, and warm community with their guides.
